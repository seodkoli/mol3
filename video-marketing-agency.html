Frequently Asked Questions

What is meant by production video?

"Production video" refers to the creation of video content, encompassing all stages from planning and shooting to editing and distribution. It involves coordinating various elements such as scriptwriting, filming, directing, and editing to produce a finished video product.

What is the difference between video production and cinematography?

Video production involves the entire process of creating video content, including planning, filming, and editing. Cinematography, on the other hand, specifically focuses on the art and technique of capturing visually appealing and aesthetically pleasing images using a camera. Cinematographers are primarily concerned with the visual aspects of filming, such as lighting, composition, and camera movement.

What do you call someone who does video production?

Someone who engages in video production is commonly referred to as a video producer or filmmaker. This individual is responsible for overseeing and coordinating all aspects of creating a video, from conceptualization to distribution.

What's the difference between production and filming?

Production encompasses all aspects of creating a video or film, including planning, scripting, filming, and post-production. Filming specifically refers to the act of capturing video footage using a camera during the production stage of the process.
